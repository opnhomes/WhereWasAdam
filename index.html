Where Was Adam?

He was there.
He said nothing.

It's time to show up.

Scroll

The Question

We skip past one detail in the oldest story we know.

When the serpent came for Eve, Adam wasn't away tending some distant field. Scripture is plain about it: he was already standing beside her. Present. Watching. Silent.

The first attack on the family God had just made didn't happen while the man was absent. It happened while he stood right there and said nothing.

"She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate." — Genesis 3:6

The Point

This isn't a story about blame. It's a question about presence.

Every man since Eden has inherited the same post Adam was given and the same silence he fell into. In a marriage, a family, a friendship, a parish, a workplace — there is always a moment that calls for a man to step in, speak up, or simply stand where he said he would stand.

Most of the damage done in this world isn't done by monsters. It's done in the gap left by good men who were there, and said nothing.

So the question isn't where Adam was. It's where you are right now.
