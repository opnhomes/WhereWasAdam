The Series

The Gap

Helping men move from where they are today to where the Lord is calling them.

Seven reflections on where men are called to show up — no longer standing in silence.

The Foundation

The first question God ever asked a man.

The story of Adam doesn't end in Genesis 3. It keeps writing itself — in every marriage, every home, every parish, every workplace where a man is present in body but absent where it counts.

This series walks through six arenas where that gap is most costly. Not as a guilt trip. As an invitation.

"Where are you?" Genesis 3:9 — God to Adam. He's still asking.
Seven Parts

The Gap Series

I

Identity

The Question We Need to be Asking

Who are you — really? Not who society says you're supposed to be. Not the roles you fill or the titles you carry. The question of identity is the first question, and it doesn't go away until it's answered. This reflection goes to the root: who God made you to be, and what it looks like to live from that truth.

"God created mankind in his image; in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them." Genesis 1:27

II

Marriage

You're Still the Man She Married

You married her to love her — fully, sacrificially, the way Christ loved the Church. That calling didn't expire. This reflection is about what it looks like to show up for your wife in a way that costs you something.

"Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her." Ephesians 5:25
Read →
III

Fatherhood

The Weight of the Word

There is no word in the human language more loaded with expectation than father. It carries everything — protection, presence, blessing, legacy. And for too many men, shame. Because somewhere between the man they intended to be and the man they became, a gap opened up. And their children are living in it.

"I kneel before the Father, from whom every family in heaven and on earth is named." Ephesians 3:14–15
Read →
IV

Friendship

Men Without Brothers

The loneliness epidemic among men is real — and largely self-inflicted. We let friendships thin out, and we call it busy. We lose the men who would have told us the truth. This reflection is about what it takes to build — or rebuild — the kind of friendship that can bear weight.

"Iron sharpens iron, and one man sharpens another." Proverbs 27:17

V

Parish & Faith

Where Are the Men?

Walk into most parishes on a Sunday morning and you'll notice something. The Church is full of women holding families together spiritually — and men sitting in the back or not sitting at all. Faith was never meant to be delegated. This is about what happens when men lead from their knees.

"As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord." Joshua 24:15

VI

Workplace

The Meeting Nobody Wanted to Run

There are moments in every professional life where something wrong is happening and everyone in the room knows it — and everyone waits for someone else to speak first. This reflection is about integrity in the ordinary: what it costs to say the true thing, and what it costs not to.

"Let what you say be simply 'Yes' or 'No'; anything more than this comes from evil." Matthew 5:37

VII

Interior Life

The War Is Inside

Every gap a man leaves in his marriage, his fatherhood, his friendships — it starts somewhere inside him. Fear. Pride. Comfort. Lust. This final reflection goes to the root: the interior life of a man who wants to show up but keeps going silent. Where the real battle is. How to fight it.

"Watch over your heart with all diligence, for from it flow the springs of life." Proverbs 4:23
